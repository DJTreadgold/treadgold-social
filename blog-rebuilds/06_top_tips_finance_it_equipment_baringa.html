Understanding Your Finance Options

When it comes to commercial equipment finance, you've got several pathways to consider:

Chattel Mortgage

A chattel mortgage is popular with businesses because the equipment serves as collateral for the loan. You own the computer equipment from day one, make fixed monthly repayments, and can claim the interest and depreciation as tax deductible expenses. This makes it a tax effective equipment financing option.

Equipment Leasing

With equipment leasing, you don't own the equipment during the life of the lease. Instead, you pay regular amounts to use it. At the end of the lease term, you might have the option to purchase the equipment, upgrade to newer technology, or return it. This is particularly useful if you want to upgrade equipment regularly without being stuck with outdated technology.

Hire Purchase

Hire Purchase lets you use the equipment while making payments over time. Once you've made your final payment, you own it outright. The loan amount is spread across the finance term with fixed monthly repayments, making it a cashflow friendly option.

The Tax Benefits Worth Knowing About

One of the biggest advantages of financing your IT equipment is the tax treatment. When you use a chattel mortgage or Hire Purchase, you can typically claim:

  1. The interest portion of your repayments as a tax deductible expense
  2. Depreciation on the equipment value
  3. GST credits on the purchase price (subject to your GST registration status)

This makes equipment finance more tax effective than many business owners initially think. Your accountant can provide specific advice based on your business structure and circumstances.

What Lenders Look For

When you apply for commercial equipment finance, lenders typically consider:

  • Your business trading history
  • Current cashflow and financial position
  • The type of equipment being financed
  • The loan amount requested
  • The equipment's value as collateral

The application process is usually straightforward, and because the equipment itself serves as security, approval can be more accessible than unsecured business finance.
